Order of the State Council of the People’s Republic of China (No. 338)

The Regulations on the Administration of Foreign Law Firms Representative Offices in China adopted at the 51st executive meeting of the State Council on December 19, 2001, are hereby promulgated, and shall come into force on January 1, 2002.

Zhu Rongji  (Premier)

December 22, 2001

Regulations on the Administration of Foreign Law Firms

Representative Offices in China

Chapter I  General Provisions

Article 1

These Regulations are enacted in accordance with the Law of the People’s Republic of China on Lawyers in order to regulate the establishment of foreign law firms representative offices in China as well as their legal services.

Article 2

These Regulations shall be applicable to the establishment of and the legal services rendered by foreign law firms representative offices in China (hereinafter referred to as representative offices).

Article 3

The representative offices and their representatives shall, when rendering legal services, abide by the laws, regulations and rules of China, strictly observe Chinese lawyers professional ethics and practice disciplines, and shall not damage Chinas national security or public benefits.

Article 4

The representative offices and their representatives shall, when rendering legal services in accordance with these Regulations, be protected by Chinese law.

Article 5

A foreign law firm shall bear the civil liabilities for the legal services rendered within the territory of China by its representative offices and the representatives.

Chapter II  Establishment, Modification and Cancellation of the Representative Offices

Article 6

Where a foreign law firm is to establish a representative office in or send representatives to China, it shall obtain the permission of the judicial administration department under the State Council.

No foreign law firm, other foreign organization or individual may render legal services within the territory of China in the name of a consulting company or in other name.

Article 7

Where a foreign law firm applies for establishing a representative office in or sending representatives to China, it shall fulfill the following conditions:

(1)   it has practiced legally in its own country, and has not been punished due to violation of lawyers professional ethics or practice disciplines;

(2)   every representative in the representative office shall be a practicing lawyer and also a member of the lawyers association of the country where he acquired his practice qualification, and has practiced as a lawyer for no less than 2 years outside the territory of China, and has not been imposed upon criminal punishments or imposed upon other punishments due to violation of lawyers professional ethics or practice disciplines; among the representatives, the chief representative shall have practiced as a lawyer for no less than 3 years outside the territory of China, and shall be a partner of the foreign law firm or a person holding the same post;

(3)   it shall be in need of establishing a representative office in China to carry out legal services.

Article 8

A foreign law firm shall, when applying for establishing a representative office in China, submit the following documents to the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government where the proposed representative office is to be domiciled:

(1)   the application letter signed by the main responsible person of the foreign law firm on establishing the representative office and sending the representatives; the name of the representative office under proposed establishment shall be (Chinese translation of the name of this law firm) ; (name of the city in China) ;

(2)   the documents attesting that the foreign law firm has been legally established in its own country;

(3)   the partnership agreement or articles of association of this foreign law firm as well as the name list of the responsible person and partners;

(4)   the powers of attorney issued by the foreign law firm to the representatives under proposed appointment in the representative office, as well as the confirmation letter stating that the chief representative under proposed appointment is a partner of this law firm or a person holding the same post;

(5)   the lawyers practice qualifications of the representatives under proposed appointment in the representative office as well as the documents attesting that the chief representative under proposed appointment has practiced as a lawyer for no less than 3 years outside the territory of China and other representatives under proposed appointment have practiced as lawyers for no less than 2 years outside the territory of China;

(6)   the documents issued by the lawyers association of the country where the foreign law firm is domiciled which attest that the representatives under proposed appointment in the representative office are members of the lawyers association of the country;

(7)   the documents issued by the lawyers administrative organ of the country where the foreign law firm is domiciled which attest that the law firm and the representatives under proposed appointment have never been imposed upon criminal punishments or imposed upon other punishments due to violation of lawyers professional ethics or practice disciplines.

The documents enumerated in the preceding paragraph shall be notarized by a public notarial office or a public notary of the applicants own country, as well as be confirmed by both the organ of the country in charge of foreign affairs or an organ authorized by the organ of the country in charge of foreign affairs and also be confirmed by the Chinese embassy or consulate stationed in that country.

The documents submitted by the foreign law firm shall be in triplicate, and their Chinese versions shall be attached.

Article 9

The judicial administration department of the People’s government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, within 3 months after receiving the application documents, complete the examination and submit the examination opinion and the documents to the judicial administration department under the State Council for check. The judicial administration department under the State Council shall make a decision within 6 months, and shall either grant a license to the representative office that is permitted to be established and practice certificates to the representatives or inform the applicant of the reason if the representative office is not permitted to be established.

Article 10

A representative office and its representatives shall not carry out the legal services provided for in these Regulations until they have, with the license and the practice certificates, completed the registration formalities in the judicial administration department of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government where the representative office is domiciled. The representative office and its representatives shall be registered once every year.

The judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall handle the registration formalities within 2 days after receiving the application for registration.

Article 11

The representative office shall, in accordance with the relevant laws and regulations, go through the formalities related to tax affairs, banking affairs and foreign exchange.

Article 12

Where a foreign law firm needs to change the name of its representative office or reduce the number of its representatives, it shall, in advance, submit the application letter signed by its main responsible person and other relevant documents to the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government where the representative office is domiciled. The practice certificates of those who will no longer be the representatives shall, upon approval by the judicial administration department under the State Council, be taken back.

Where a representative office is under merger, division or is to increase newly appointed representatives, it shall go through the permission formalities in accordance with the provisions in these Regulations related to the procedures on the establishment of representative offices.

Article 13

Where a representative in a representative office is under any of the following circumstances, the judicial administration department under the State Council shall revoke his practice permit and take back his practice certificate, and the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall accordingly cancel his practice registration:

(1)   his lawyer license in his own country has become ineffective;

(2)   his qualification as a representative has been cancelled by the foreign law firm which he belongs to;

(3)   his practice certificate or the license of the representative office where he works has been revoked.

Article 14

Where a representative office is under any of the following circumstances, the judicial administration department under the State Council shall revoke its practice permit and take back its license, and the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall accordingly cancel its practice registration:

(1)   the foreign law firm which it belongs to has been dissolved or cancelled;

(2)   the foreign law firm which it belongs to applies to be cancelled;

(3)   it has lost the conditions provided for in Article 7 of these Regulations;

(4)   its license has been revoked in accordance with the law.

A representative office that is cancelled in accordance with the preceding paragraph shall carry out the liquidation in accordance with the law; and it shall not transfer its properties out of the territory of China before its debts have been paid off.

 

Chapter III  Scope of Business and Rules

Article 15

A representative office and its representatives may only be engaged in the following activities that do not involve the affairs on Chinese law:

(1)   to provide the parties concerned with consultation in respect of the laws of the country where the lawyers in this foreign law firm are permitted to practice as lawyers, as well as consultation related to international treaties and international practices;

(2)   to be entrusted by the parties or Chinese law firms to handle legal affairs in the country where the lawyers in this foreign law firm are permitted to practice as lawyers;

(3)   to represent foreign parties to entrust Chinese law firms to handle the affairs on Chinese law;

(4)   to keep long-term contractual entrustment relationship with Chinese law firms to handle legal affairs;

(5)   to provide information on the influences to Chinese legal environment.

A representative office may, as stipulated in the agreement with a Chinese law firm, directly propose requirements to the lawyers in the entrusted Chinese law firm.

A representative office and its representatives shall not be engaged in the legal services or profit-making activities other than those provided for in Paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article.

Article 16

A representative office shall not employ Chinese practicing lawyers; and the assistants employed by it shall not provide the parties concerned with legal services.

Article 17

A representative office and its representatives shall not conduct the following acts in their practice activities:

(1)   to provide false evidence, conceal facts or to, with promise of gain, intimidate another into providing false evidence, concealing facts or induce him to do so, or to obstruct the opposing party from obtaining the evidence lawfully;

(2)   to accept the properties or other benefits of the parties concerned by taking advantage of the legal services;

(3)   to divulge the commercial secrets or individual privacy of the parties concerned.

Article 18

No representative in a representative office shall simultaneously hold the post of representative in two or more representative offices.

Article 19

A representative in a representative office shall reside within the territory of China for a period not less than 6 months every year, or he shall not be registered in the next year.

Article 20

A representative office may charge the parties concerned for the legal services provided for in these Regulations. However, the fees charged must be settled within the territory of China.

 

Chapter IV  Supervision

Article 21

The judicial administration department under the State Council and the judicial administration department of the People’s government of each province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, on the basis of their duties, be responsible for the supervision of the representative offices and their representatives.

Article 22

A representative office shall, on March 31 of each year, submit the copies of its license and the practice certificates of its representatives as well as the following inspection documents of the last year to the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government where it is domiciled for annual inspection:

(1)   the information on its legal services activities, including that on its entrusting Chinese law firms to handle legal affairs;

(2)   the annual financial statements of the representative office that have been audited by an accounting firm, and the documents on its settlement within the territory of China and its payment of taxes in accordance with the law;

(3)   the information on the change of its representatives and on its employment of Chinese assistants;

(4)   the information on the residence within the territory of China of its representatives;

(5)   the information on its registration and on the registration of its representatives;

(6)   other information on its implementation of the obligations provided for in these Regulations.

The judicial administration department of the People’s government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, after completing the annual inspection on the representative offices within its own competence, submit the inspection opinions to the judicial administration department under the State Council for record.

Article 23

The judicial administration department of the People’s government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government must, when collecting fees for the registration of the representative offices and their representatives, as well as for the annual inspection of the representative offices, strictly execute the same standards as those for Chinese law firms and practicing lawyers, which have been checked by the price administration department under the State Council. The fees collected must be totally turned in to the State treasury.

The judicial administration department of the People’s government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall, when executing an administrative penalty of fine, separate the decision on the fine from the collection of the fine in accordance with the relevant laws and administrative regulations. All the fines collected and the illegal proceeds confiscated in accordance with the law must be turned in to the State treasury.

Chapter V  Legal Liabilities

Article 24

Where a representative office or any of its representative endangers Chinas national security or public security or obstructs the administration of public order, it/he shall be investigated for criminal liabilities in accordance with the provisions in the Criminal Law on the crime of endangering national security, endangering public security or obstructing the administration of public order, and the judicial administration department under the State Council shall revoke the license of the representative office or the practice certificate of the representative; where the case is not serious enough for a criminal punishment to be imposed, it/he shall be imposed upon an administrative penalty for public security in accordance with the law, and the judicial administration department under the State Council shall revoke the license of the representative office or the practice certificate of the representative.

Article 25

Where a representative office or any of its representative violates Article 15 of these Regulations by illegally carrying out legal services or other profit-making activities, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall order it/him to cease the practice within a time limit; if the case is serious, the judicial administration department under the State Council shall revoke the license of the representative office or the practice certificate of the representative.

Where a representative office or any of its representative commits any of the illegal acts enumerated in the preceding paragraph, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall confiscate the illegal proceeds, and impose a fine of 50,000 Yuan to 200,000 Yuan respectively upon the chief representative and each of the directly liable representatives.

Article 26

Where a representative office is under any of the following circumstances, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall give it a warning and order it to make a rectification within a time limit; if the case is serious, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall order it to cease the practice within a time limit; where it has not made a rectification after the time limit, the judicial administration department under the State Council shall revoke its license:

(1)   it employs Chinese practicing lawyers, or the employed assistants are engaged in legal services;

(2)   it does not settle the fees collected from its legal services within the territory of China;

(3)   it does not submit the annual inspection documents and does not accept the annual inspection in time, or fails to pass the annual inspection.

Where a representative office commits any of the illegal acts enumerated in Item (2) of the preceding paragraph, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall impose upon it a fine of 1 time to 3 times of the amount to be settled within the territory of China.

Article 27

Where a representative office or any of its representative is under any of the following circumstances, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of a province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall give it/him a warning, confiscate its/his illegal proceeds; and shall, if the case is serious, order it/him to cease the practice within a time limit, and impose upon it/him a fine of 20,000 Yuan to 100,000 Yuan:

(1)   to simultaneously hold the post of representative in two or more representative offices;

(2)   to divulge the commercial secrets or individual privacy of the parties concerned;

(3)   to accept the properties or other benefits of the parties concerned by taking advantage of the legal services.

Article 28

Where a representative office under cancellation transfers its properties out of the territory of China before its debts have been paid off, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall order it to return the transferred properties to pay off the debts; if such transfer seriously damages the benefits of any other person, the chief representative and the directly liable persons shall be investigated for criminal liabilities in accordance with the provisions in the Criminal Law on the crime of concealing properties; where the case is not serious enough for a criminal punishment to be imposed, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall impose a fine of 50,000 Yuan to 300,000 Yuan upon the representative office, and a fine of 20,000 Yuan to 100,000 Yuan respectively upon the chief representative and each of the directly liable persons.

Article 29

Where any representative in a representative office provides false evidence, conceals facts or intimidates another with promise of gain into providing false evidence or concealing facts or induces him to do so, he shall be investigated for criminal liabilities in accordance with the provisions in the Criminal Law on the crime of impairing testimony, and the judicial administration department under the State Council shall revoke his practice certificate.

Article 30

Where a foreign law firm, a foreign lawyer or any other foreign organization or individual renders legal services within the territory of China without authorization, or a representative office or a representative whose practice permit has been revoked continues rendering legal services within the territory of China, the judicial administration department of the People’s government of the province, autonomous region or municipality directly under the Central Government shall lay a ban on such legal services, confiscate the illegal proceeds and impose a fine of 50,000 Yuan to 300,000 Yuan upon the violator.

Article 31

Where the license of a representative office has been revoked in accordance with the law, the foreign law firm that it belongs to shall not apply for establishing a second representative office in China within 5 years. Where the practice certificate of a representative in a representative office has been revoked in accordance with the law, he shall not hold the post of representative in any representative office in China within 5 years.

Where a representative in a representative office is sentenced to a criminal punishment in accordance with the law for endangering Chinas national security or public security or obstructing the administration of public order, the law firm which the representative office belongs to shall no longer apply for establishing a second representative office in China, and the said representative shall no longer hold the post of representative in any representative office in China for life.

Article 32

Where any functionary in the judicial administration department commits any of the following illegal acts, the liable person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be imposed upon such administrative sanctions as the recording of a demerit, the recording of a major demerit or demotion in accordance with the law:

(1)   not to examine or check the attestation documents of the representatives offices under proposed establishment and of the representative under proposed appointment in accordance with the conditions provided for in these Regulations;

(2)   not to handle the registration for or the annual inspection on the representative offices in accordance with these Regulations;

(3)   not to collect the fees in accordance with the items or standards provided for by the State.

Article 33

Where any functionary in the judicial administration department commits any of the following illegal acts, the liable person in charge and other directly liable persons shall be imposed upon such administrative sanctions as demotion, dismissal from office or discharge in accordance with the law:

(1)   to decide to issue a license to a representative office under proposed establishment or a practice certificate to a representative under proposed appointment when the said representative office or representative does not fulfill the conditions provided for in these Regulations;

(2)   to accept properties or seek personal benefits by taking advantage of his office;

(3)   to violate these Regulations by not revoking the practice permit of a representative office or a representative which should be revoked or by not taking back its license or his practice certificate which should be taken back or by not canceling the practice registration which should be cancelled;

(4)   not to issue a fine receipt or not to write down the authentic amount when collecting a fine;

(5)   not to execute the system of separating collection from the turning-in of fines or not to totally turn in the fees or fines collected or the illegal proceeds confiscated in accordance with the law to the State treasury;

(6)   not to timely investigate the acts of a representative office or its representative in violation of these Regulations;

(7)   to have committed the act of not strictly executing the law or other acts of abusing power, thus causing serious consequences.

In case of any of the illegal acts enumerated in the preceding paragraph, thus causing serious losses to public properties or the benefits of the State or of the people, criminal liabilities shall be investigated in accordance with the provisions in the Criminal Law on the crime of abusing power, neglecting duty or accepting bribes.

Chapter VI  Supplementary Provisions

Article 34

The measures on the administration of the representative offices established in the inland of China by the law firms in Chinas single customs zone shall be separately enacted by the judicial administration department under the State Council in accordance with these Regulations.

Article 35

These Regulations shall come into force on January 1, 2002. The foreign law firms representative offices in China which have already started their practice and the representatives who have already practiced upon permission by the judicial administration department under the State Council before these Regulations come into force shall, within 90 days as of the date when these Regulations come into force, re-apply for going through the approval formalities in accordance with these Regulations.
