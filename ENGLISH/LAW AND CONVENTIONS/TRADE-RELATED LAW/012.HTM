PRC PROVISIONS ON PLACE OF ORIGIN OF EXPORT GOODS

(adopted by the Executive Meeting of the State Council on Feb. 28,  1992 and issued as Order No. 94 on March 8)  
Article 1. These Provisions are stipulated for  improving the administration of the place of origin of export  goods to promote foreign economic relations and trade.  
Article 2. The PRC Certificate of Origin (COO) of Export Goods  is the document to prove that the related export goods has the  People's Republic of China as the original manufacturing place.  
Article 3. The government departments in charge of foreign  economic relations and trade shall exercise unified supervision and  control over the identification of the place of origin of export goods  across the country.  
Departments in charge of foreign economic relations and trade of  people's governments in provinces, autonomous regions and  municipalities directly under the administration of the central  government shall give coordination to such work in their own  administrative areas.  
Article 4. According to the regulations of the government  departments in charge of foreign economic relations and trade, a COO,  shall be signed and issued by the local offices of the  import-and export-merchandise government inspection departments set  up in localities, the China Council for the Promotion of International  Trade and its branches, as well as other setups assigned by the  departments in charge of foreign economic relations and trade.  
Article 5. Established according to law in the PRC territory,  those enterprises which enjoy the right to engage in foreign  trade and the enterprises which engage in processing or  assembling products with raw materials or parts provided by foreign  clients, or which accept orders for goods made to foreign buyers'  designs, or extend compensation trade, and those enterprises with  overseas investment, may apply to the authorized organs specified in  Article 4 for obtaining a COO.  
Article 6. The export goods which meet any one of the  following conditions shall have the People's Republic of China  as the original manufacturing place.  
(A) Products entirely made or manufactured in the territory of the  People's Republic of China, including  
1. Minerals extracted from the PRC territory and continental shelf;  
2. Plants harvested or collected in the PRC territory and their  products;  
3. Animals bred and raised in the PRC territory and their products;  
4. Products hunted or caught in the PRC territory;  
5. Marine and other products obtained by PRC ships or other  instruments from the sea and their processed goods;  
6. Scraps and waste materials recycled in the course of  manufacturing or processing in the PRC territory and other waste and  used articles collected in the PRC territory;  
7. Products entirely made from the above-mentioned products or from  other non-imported raw materials in the PRC territory.  
(B) Products that are mainly and finally made or processed in  the PRC territory with partially or entirely imported raw materials,  spare parts and components, resulting in an essential change in  their appearance, nature, form, or use. According to the  principle of focusing on manufacturing and processing and  supplementing it with structural ratio, the government departments  in charge of foreign economic relations and trade after consulting  with related State Council departments shall make and modify the  manufacturing or processing operation statements.  
Article 7. Export goods listed in an application for obtaining  a COO must meet the conditions of the original manufacturing place.  Otherwise the signatory organ shall refuse to sign and issue the COO.  
Article 8. The government departments in charge of foreign  economic relations and trade shall work out the procedures for  applying and signing the COO.  
Article 9. The government departments in charge of foreign  economic relations and trade, or by suggestions of foreign trade  departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous  regions or municipalities directly under the administration of the  central government, may circulate a notice of criticism of an  enterprise, or temporarily suspend granting it OCC, or cancel its  right for application, if the enterprise violates the Provisions hereby  and commits one of the following offenses:  
1. Submitting false documents, trying to get the COOs by cheating;  
2. Forging or tampering with a COO;  
3. Illegally transferring a COO.  
Persons in charge of the enterprise and those directly responsible  for the above offenses shall be subject to administrative punishment.  Serious cases shall be punished by bringing them to criminal court.  
Article 10. If a signatory organ violates the Provisions  hereby in signing and issuing a COO or refusing to sign on issue it,  the government departments in charge of foreign economic  relations and trade, or by suggestions of the foreign trade  departments of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous  regions, or municipalities directly under the administration of  the Central Government, may circulate a notice of criticism of  this signatory organ or temporarily suspend its right to sign and issue  the COOs.  
Personnel of the signatory organ who bend the law for personal  benefit, or abuse their power, or neglect their duty, shall  be subject to administrative punishment. Serious cases involving  crime will be brought before the court.  
Article 11. The GSP COOs shall be issued according to  provisions on place of origin of the country providing the general  perferences.  
If there are special provisions on the signing and issuing of  the COOs defined in bilateral agreements between the governments  of the People's Republic of China and other countries, the COOs  shall be issued according to the special provisions.  
Article 12. According to these Provisions, the government  departments in charge of foreign economic relations and trade shall  work out rules for implementation of those provisions.  
Article 13. The government departments in charge of foreign  economic relations and trade are authorized to interpret these  Provisions.  
Article 14. These Provisions shall come into force as from  May 1, 1992.
