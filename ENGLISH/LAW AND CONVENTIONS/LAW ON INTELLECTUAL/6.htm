Order of the State Council of the People’s Republic of China (No.345)

The Regulations on the Protection of Olympic Symbols have been approved at the 54th executive meeting of the State Council on Jan. 30, 2002 and are hereby promulgated, and shall come into force on April 1, 2002.

Premier Zhu Rongji

Feb. 4, 2002

Regulations on the Protection of Olympic Symbols

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated in order to intensify the protection of Olympic symbols, to guarantee the lawful rights and interests of the right owners of Olympic symbols and to safeguard the dignity of the Olympic Games.

Article 2 The “Olympic symbols” as used in these Regulations shall refer to:

1)    The Olympic five-ring pattern symbol, Olympic flag, Olympic motto, Olympic mark and Olympic song of the International Olympic Committee;

2)    The exclusive names such as “Olympic”, “Olympia”, “Olympic Games” and the abbreviations thereof;

3)    The name, mark and symbol of the China Olympic Committee;

4)    The name, mark and symbol of the Bidding Committee of Beijing 2008 Olympic Games;

5)    The name and mark of the Organizing Committee of the 29th Olympic Games, the mascot, game song and slogans of the 29th Olympic Games, the “Beijing 2008”, the 29th Olympic Games and the abbreviation thereof;

6)    The other symbols related to the 29th Olympic Games as provided for by the Olympic Charter and the Contract of the Host City of the 29th Olympic Games.

Article 3 The right owners of Olympic symbols as used in these Regulations shall refer to the International Olympic Committee, the China Olympic Committee and the Organizing Committee of the 29th Olympic Games.

The division of rights among the International Olympic Committee, the China Olympic Committee and the Organizing Committee of the 29th Olympic Games shall be determined according to the Olympic Charter and the Contract of the Host City of the 29th Olympic Games.

Article 4 The right owners of Olympic symbols shall own the exclusive rights to the Olympic symbols according to these Regulations.

No one may use the Olympic symbols for commercial purposes (including potential commercial purposes, hereinafter the same) without the permission of the right owners of the Olympic symbols.

Article 5 The “use for commercial purposes” as used in these Regulations shall refer to use the Olympic symbols for profit through the following ways:

1)    Using the Olympic symbols on commodities, commodity package or containers, and the documents of commodity trade;

2)    Using the Olympic symbols in service projects;

3)    Using the Olympic symbols in advertisements and publicity, commercial exhibitions, business performances and other commercial activities;

4)    Selling, importing or exporting commodities that contain the Olympic symbols;

5)       Manufacturing or selling the Olympic symbols;

6)    Other acts of using the Olympic symbols that may lead others to believe that there are sponsoring or other supportive relations between the right owners of the Olympic symbols and the actor.

Article 6 The department of industry and commerce administration under the State Council shall be responsible for the protection of the Olympic symbols of the whole country according to the provisions of these Regulations.

The local departments of industry and commerce administration at the level of county and above shall be responsible for the protection of the Olympic symbols within their respective administrative areas.

Article 7 The right owners of the Olympic symbols shall report the Olympic symbols to the department of industry and commerce administration under the State Council for record, which shall make the public announcements.

Article 8 Those using the Olympic symbols with the permission of the right owners of the Olympic symbols shall make the contracts for licensed use with the right owners of the Olympic symbols. Among which, those using the Olympic symbols as prescribed in items 1) and 2) of Article 2 of these Regulations shall make contracts with the International Olympic Committee and the agencies authorized or approved thereby; those using the Olympic symbols as prescribed in item 3) of Article 2 of these Regulations shall make contracts with the China Olympic Committee; those using the Olympic symbols as prescribed in items 4), 5), 6) of Article 2 of these Regulations shall make contracts with the Organizing Committee of the 29th Olympic Games before Dec. 31, 2008. The right owners of the Olympic symbols shall report the contracts for licensed use to the department of industry and commerce administration for record.

Where contracts for licensed use are made according to the preceding paragraph, the licensed user may only use the Olympic symbols within the areas and periods as agreed upon in the contracts.

Article 9 Those who have used the Olympic symbols according to law before the implementation of these Regulations may continue to use within the original scope.

Article 10 Any unauthorized use of Olympic symbols for commercial purposes without permission of the right owners of Olympic symbols shall be an infringement upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols, where any dispute arises, the parties concerned shall settle the dispute through negotiation; where the parties refuse to negotiate or the negotiation fails, the right owners of Olympic symbols or the interested parties may bring a lawsuit before the people’s court, or they may request the department of industry and commerce administration for handling. When the department of industry and commerce administration handle the dispute, if it holds that the an infringement has been constituted, it shall order immediate stop of the infringement, confiscate and destroy the infringing commodities and the tools specially used to manufacture the infringing commodities or to manufacture the Olympic symbols for commercial purposes without authorization, and confiscate the illegal gains if there is any, and may impose a fine of not more than 5 times of the illegal gains concurrently; or a fine of not more than 50, 000 Yuan if there is no illegal gain.

If any party refuses to accept the decision of solving the dispute, it may bring a lawsuit before the people’s court within 15 days from the day of receiving the notification of handling according to the Administrative Procedure Law of the People’s Republic of China; if the infringer neither brings a lawsuit nor performs the decision, the department of industry and commerce administration may apply to the people’s court to enforce the decision. The department of industry and commerce administrative handling the case may, according to the request of the parties, mediate over the amount of compensation for the infringement upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols; if the mediation fails, the parties may bring a lawsuit before the people’s court according to the Civil Procedure Law of the People’s Republic of China.

Where anyone commits swindles, etc. by using the Olympic symbols and violates the criminal law, the criminal responsibilities shall be investigated for according to the provisions of the criminal law on the crime of swindle or other crimes.

Article 11 The department of industry and commerce administration shall have the right to investigate and punish the acts infringing upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols.

The department of industry and commerce administration may exercise the following authorities when, based on the obtained evidence or report of violation of law, investigating and punishing the acts suspected to infringing upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols:

1)    Inquiring the relevant parties, investigate the information related to the infringement upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols;

2)    Consulting, copying the contracts, vouchers, account books and other relevant materials related to the infringing activities;

3)    Making on-spot examinations of the places where the parties are suspected to conduct the activities infringing upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols；

4)    Examining the articles related to the infringing activities; seizing or distraining the articles that have been proved to infringe upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols by evidence.

When the department of industry and commerce administration exercises the authorities as provided for in the preceding paragraph, the parties shall give support and assistance, and may not refuse or frustrate.

Article 12 Where import and export goods are suspected to infringe upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols, the customs shall investigate and punish according to the limit of authorities and the procedures as provided for by the Customs Law of the People’s Republic of China and the Regulations of the Republic of China on the Customs Protection of Intellectual Property Rights.

Article 13 The amount of compensation for the infringement upon the exclusive rights to an Olympic symbol shall be determined according to the losses caused to the right owner due to the infringement or the profits gained by the infringer through the infringement, including the reasonable expenses paid to stop the infringing acts; where it is difficult to determine the losses of the infringed or the profits obtained by the infringer, it may be determined in reference to the fees for licensed use of that Olympic symbol.

Any one who sells the commodities infringing upon the exclusive rights to Olympic symbols without knowing the fact may, if he can prove that he obtained the commodities according to law and may point out the provider, bear no responsibilities for compensation.

Article 14 Apart from being protected according to these Regulations, the Olympic symbols may also seek protection according to the provisions of the laws and regulations such as the Copyright of the People’s Republic of China, the Trademark Law of the People’s Republic of China, the Patent Law of the People’s Republic of China, and the Regulations on the Administration of Specials Symbols, etc.

Article 15 These Regulations shall come into force on April 1, 2002.
